Project Title: Survey of Texas feedyard employees

Project Description:It has been proven in dairy, poultry and swine that stockperson personality, attitude and behavior strongly effects animal welfare in production settings. In the beef cattle industry, less research has been done in this area. To begin to better understand the dynamics of the human-animal relationship between beef cattle, we need to better understanding the working population in cattle feed yards. We have created a survey to administer to cattle stock people to better understand their perception of animals, their level of job satisfaction and socio-economic background. We will be administering the survey April 2018.

Project Title: Effects of different diet compositions on tongue rolling behavior in beef cattle

Project Description: This study is one part of our effort to understand the connection between diet and non-nutritive oral stereotypies in cattle. Specifically, the cattle in this study are fed different levels of roughage in the diet. We are using video decoding to record the duration and frequency of tongue rolling behaviors to determine if there is a dietary based motivation to perform this behavior.
